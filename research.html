Hongzhu Wang

Principle Researcher of Subject Subject Group

Research Areas

  • Systematics and biogeography of invertebrates, including free-living Nematoda, aquatic and terrestrial microdrile Oligochaeta (Annelida) and freshwater Mollusca.

  • Ecology of macrofauna and meiofauna: community ecology, food-web, biomonitoring, environmental requirements of ecosystem health.

  • River-floodplain ecology: evaluate effects of water projects on rivers and floodplains, assess environmental flow requirements, and provide integrated strategy for restoration and protection of river systems.

  • Predictive limnology and regional limnology: quantitatively reveal macroecological rules of river and lakes, and establish regional expert systems of ecosystem managements.

Recent Achievements

Click here for more details

  • Described over 40 new species of Oligochaeta, and one new genus of freshwater Nemertea; revised one genus of marine oligochaetes, Doliodrilus.

  • Studied pattern detection and ecotoxicological responses of aquatic oligochaetes by the method of Artificial Neural Network.

  • Analyzed food composition of benthic animals in Yangtze lakes, and compiled a series of food webs, i.e.Â connective web, ingestion web, dietary overlap web, energy flow web.

  • Established key-time models of submersed macrophytes, abundance models of periphytic gastropods, and optimal-stocking models of Chinese mitten crab in Yangtze lakes, through predictive limnological approach

  • Revealed ecological effects of river-lake isolation, and devised a preliminary strategy to restore Yangtze river-floodplain ecosystem in a holistic way.
